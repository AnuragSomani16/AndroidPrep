Become indistinguishable from a 5-year senior in 48 hours.

A complete, opinionated study system. Senior-signal phrases are highlighted on every page — drop them naturally and the interviewer thinks "ohh, this guy knows his stuff." Each topic is built around concept → why it exists → idiomatic example → senior framing → common pitfall.

How to use this site

1. Read each page out loud once. 2. Use the Mock Interview page to drill answers — filter by difficulty, expand answers only after attempting. 3. Memorize the senior-signal phrases; they're your tells. 4. Practice the STAR stories out loud, timed at 60–90 sec each.

The 4 senior-signal pillars

PILLAR 1

Reasoning over recall

Juniors recite. Seniors reason from first principles, name tradeoffs, and pre-empt edge cases. Every code snippet here is paired with "why" and "what breaks it."

PILLAR 2

Concrete numbers

"Reduced cold start from 2.4s to 1.1s on low-end TVs" beats "improved performance" every time. The STAR stories have your numbers.

PILLAR 3

Tradeoff fluency

Every architectural choice has a cost. "MVI is great when…" "I'd avoid Hilt's @Singleton when…" Show you choose, don't follow.

PILLAR 4

Production scars

Talk about the bug you found in AdMob's SDK. The OOM you traced. The race the test caught. Scars beat resumes.

Don'ts (read these now)

Don't memorize verbatim — interviewers smell rehearsal. Don't pretend deep MVI experience if probed; scope your claim ("I used MVI patterns in our state-heavy player screens"). Don't open AI for the first hour of any coding practice — your fingers need to remember Kotlin without help.

Day-of checklist (Monday morning)
